LOINC-encoding and Computerizing Nursing and Mental Health Assessment Instruments

  • INTRODUCTION / BACKGROUND
    • Assumptions
      • Lack of data standards can result in sub-optimal, overly costly care
      • We can create reference terminology for nursing and mental health instruments
    • What are barriers to doing this?
      • Standards
        • Coding standard for individual items
        • Coding standard for individual instruments comprised of items
        • Coding standard for validations & calculations within instrument scope
        • Coding standard for representing modules and associated presentation features
        • Data transmission standards for individual items, collections of them, and instruments
      • Terminologies
        • Semantic model showing relationship between item questions and response sets
      • Prioritization
        • Triaging of instruments needing to be coded
        • Reaching consensus on how to prioritize
      • Database Modeling
        • Scalable, maintainable, normalized databases for storing items and instruments
      • Development
        • Easy tools for authoring, reviewing and validating items and instruments
        • Tools for normalizing
        • Fuzzy matching tools for comparing items
        • Tools for comparing item questions and response sets with UMLS
      • Statistics
        • Tools to psychometrically re-validate instruments
      • Clerical
        • Staff to convert from paper versions to standard format
      • Legal
        • Copyright transfer and/or attribution
        • Distribution policy (akin to UMLS)
        • Tools for identifying copyrighted items used in other instruments
      • ...
    • How many instruments are there?
      • Over 4000 commercially available
      • Department of Child Psychiatry has 434 available
      • ~ Unpublished ones

      • Handbook of Psychiatric Measures (c) 2000
      • Mental Measurements Yearbook
      • Health and Psychosocial Instruments (HaPI) (Behavioral Measurement Database Service)
      • PSYINDEXplus with TestFinder
      • Department of Child Psychiatry
