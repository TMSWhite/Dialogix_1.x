The Automated Morningness-Eveningness Questionnaire (AutoMEQ-SA)      
NOTES

The AutoMEQ was modified from: Horne JA, Östberg O. A self-assessment questionnaire to determine morningness-eveningness in human circadian rhythms. International Journal of Chronobiology 1976;4:97-110.(abstract)

The predictive algorithms are based on data for 71 patients with winter depression, presented in: Terman M, Terman JS. Morningness-eveningness, circadian phase and the timing of sleep in patients with seasonal affective disorder. Society for Light Treatment and Biological Rhythms Abstracts 2001;13:29.(abstract)

For general information about light therapy, see www.cet.org. For information about treatment apparatus used in the clinical trials underlying the predictive algorithms of the AutoMEQ, see www.sphereone.com. For information about participation in clinical trials at Columbia-Presbyterian Medical Center, see www.light-and-ion-therapy.org.

The AutoMEQ was designed by Michael Terman, Ph.D., Thomas M. White, M.D., M.S., and Joy Jacobs, J.D., New York State Psychiatric Institute (lightion@pi.cpmc.columbia.edu) under Grant MH42931 from the National Institute of Mental Health.
